About Me

My name is Travis Guiley. I'm currently the owner of a small retail business that sells card games and board games. I graduated from Eastern University in Cheney, WA with a bachelor's degree in Finance and Management Info Systems with a minor in Economics. For the first 7 years of my career I worked as a business/financial analyst. I then decided to follow a passion of mine and open a retail game store, but running a small business is never easy. You can plan all you like, but something will always go wrong. It has been a wonderful experience and I learned a lot, but it became time to try something different.

As an analyst, I became an Excel Guru. No matter how far I got in my career there was always someone who "just wanted a spreadsheet." As our team capabilities evolved with the times, spreadsheets became one of the most frustrating parts of my job. We had brought Google BigQuery online and the amount of data we had at our fingertips was astounding. Coupled with tools like Tableau we could take all that data and every single day you could go in and see how your previous day performed without even having to send an email. But still, there was always someone that wanted a spreadsheet.

Since I couldn't get away from the spreadsheets, I did the next best thing. I spent a whole lot of time working in SQL. The spreadsheets could not handle the raw data, there was just too much. Between the need for spreadsheets to have smaller datasets, and all the new Tableau dashboards we were building, I got pretty good at SQL. It became the favorite part of my job. Just like any programmer would, I spent a lot of time scowling at my screen, searching StackOverflow forums, and when all else failed running over to our Advanced Analytics team (the team that build our BigQuery tables). But I loved every minute of it. I could get absolutely lost in the code. I love a good puzzle, and every new query was the latest puzzle to solve.

I've moved from Eagle River, Alaska to Spokane, Washington (wasn't far enough south) to Las Vegas, NV and have seen more of the world already at 35 than some people ever will in their entire lives. I've been to 15 countries and 16 different states within the US searching for where I want to live and for the answer to the famous "want do you want to be when you grow up" question. I might not yet know where I want to live forever, but I'm confident I've finally figured out the answer to that famous question. I want to write code. It can be games, websites, or applications. Android or Windows or the next greatest thing it doesn't matter. I just want to solve my next puzzle.
